Dr. Norman Borlaug

the man saved a billion lives

This video is a tribute to Nobel prize winner, Norman Borlaug, a plant breeder who touched over a billion lives with his determination to stop world hunger. This story features the challenges and successes that Borlaug and his team faced as they developed new varieties, pioneered new methods and refused to give up. A great example of the impact that one person can have on the world, this story is truly inspiring.

Dr. Norman Borlaug (1914–2009) was an American agronomist, humanitarian, and Nobel laureate who pioneered the Green Revolution. His research on high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties helped save over a billion lives by preventing famine in many developing nations. His lifelong mission was to use science and education to end hunger.

Work

  • 1944–1960 – Mexico Wheat Program: Led research that produced thousands of disease-resistant wheat strains, helping Mexico achieve self-sufficiency.
  • 1960s – Green Revolution in South Asia: Introduced high-yield wheat to India and Pakistan, dramatically increasing food production and averting famine.
  • 1970 – Nobel Peace Prize: Awarded for contributions to world peace through increased food supply.
  • 1980s – Work in Africa: Helped African countries improve their maize and sorghum yields.
  • 2000s – Advocacy: Promoted sustainable agriculture and responsible biotechnology to meet global food demand.

Resume

Education

  • B.S., Forestry – University of Minnesota (1937)
  • Ph.D., Genetics and Plant Pathology – University of Minnesota (1942)

Key Positions

  • Research Scientist – Rockefeller Foundation, Mexico Wheat Program
  • Director – International Maize and Wheat Improvement Center (CIMMYT)
  • Distinguished Professor – Texas A&M University (1984–2009)

Major Achievements

  • Developed high-yield, rust-resistant wheat varieties that revolutionized agriculture.
  • Received the Nobel Peace Prize (1970), the Presidential Medal of Freedom, and the Congressional Gold Medal.
  • Advocated for modern agricultural technologies to combat hunger.
